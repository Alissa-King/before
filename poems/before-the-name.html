Before the Name

VII

There's a thing that doesn't break.

I know because I've tried —
not intentionally, not with the part of me
that understood what I was doing,
but in the years I kept mistaking the floor for the wall
and walking face-first into what I thought was a way through —

and something held.

Not something I'd earned or built
in the careful seasons —
not discipline, not distance,
not the quiet I constructed
in the years I learned to need
a little less
out loud —

something older than the earning.
Something that was there
before I had a word for here.

I want to call it soul.
But the word feels too finished —
too clean, too much like an answer
for something that doesn't answer questions.

It just stays.

Warm in the chest
like a stone that's been sitting in the sun —
not a fire,
not the burning I've confused with being alive —
just warmth.
The unremarkable,
unasked-for,
constant warmth
of something that does not go out.

And I've tried to understand what it wants.
Gave it grief. Gave it joy.
Gave it the years I barely made it through
and the years I didn't even know I was missing —
every self I grew into and then outgrew,
every shape I was sure was the last shape
until the day it wasn't —
every necessary becoming
I mistook for arrival —

and it doesn't prefer.

It holds them all the same.

Even that, it says.
Even that. And that. And that.

Not forgiving, not forgetting,
not making meaning out of the mess —
just holding,
the way water holds what falls into it
without deciding
what deserves to float.

That's the thing about the witness.
The one that lives below the living.

It never asked me to compress the whole of me
into something small enough to carry across —
never loved the approximation of me
instead of the fact.
Never reached toward me
and quietly, kindly,
chosen the nearness
instead of the truth.

And in the wrong-o'clock rooms —
the ones with no time worth reading,
the ones where the hour stops mattering
because whatever is happening in them
has been happening forever —
the ones where I spread the whole of me out
like something recovered,
like evidence of a life
I wasn't sure I'd make it through —

it was there.
Not fixing.
Not explaining the rubble.
Not offering meaning in exchange for the mess.

Just there.

A deep, quiet yes
that doesn't mean good
and doesn't mean brave
and doesn't mean I forgive you —

it means:
I see you.
I was here before the first story
you told yourself about yourself
and I'll be here after the last one
finally
falls apart.

And here's the thing —
the thing I've had to learn
the hard way,
the long way,
the only way I seem to learn anything —

I don't have to earn it.

The thing that watches doesn't grade you.
Doesn't score the seasons.
Doesn't keep a ledger
of what you failed to carry
and what went dark without a word.

It just witnesses.

And maybe that's the only truly unconditional
I will ever know —

not someone choosing to stay
when leaving would have been easier,
not forgiveness tendered after the flood,
not love that reached all the way across
and landed somewhere near —

just the ancient, quiet thing
below the sternum
that never decided.

Never made a choice.

Was always,
already,
home.
