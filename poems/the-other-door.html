The Other Door

II

0:00

What if you've already done this?

What if dying is just
the other door —
the one you came through
before you knew
you were coming,
before you had a name for the light
or a word for the cold
or a body to hold
all that arriving in?

You weren't afraid then.
Think about that.
You weren't afraid then.

You crossed the whole impossible distance
between nothing and here —
between silence and first breath and fear —
and you didn't even know to grieve it,
didn't know to clutch the dark and say
wait,
wait, I need another minute,
I don't know what's on the other side —

you just came.

And maybe that's what dying is.
Not an ending.
Not the lights going out in an empty house.
Maybe it's the forgetting
you already survived once —
the same crossing,
the same unmarked water,
the same darkness that turned out to hold you
before you were anything
that could be held.

We call it death
like it's the first time.

Like we haven't been
to the edge of the everything
and stepped off
and arrived
and forgotten
and become
and loved
and lost
and clutched this life
like it's the only one we know how to live —

because it is.
Because we forgot.

But the soul remembers
what the mind can't hold —
that before is after,
that the cold is also warmth arriving,
that the last breath out
is just the first breath in
for whatever comes next,

whatever you become
when you stop being this —

this specific, suffering,
beautiful, unfinished,
reaching you.

And it won't be loss.

It'll be the way it felt
before you knew you'd arrived —
weightless,
formless,
and completely
unafraid.
