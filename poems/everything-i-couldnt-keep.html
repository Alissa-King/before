Everything I Couldn't Keep

IV

There's a self I outgrew still standing in the shed skin,
in the husk of who I was before the body chose to begin
the long shape down and step out clean — and she isn't sorrow,
isn't ghost, she's just the hollow held in the exact pose
of the last day she was worn.

And I'd like to say I shed her like a snake does, without grief,
no backward glance, no mourning for the casing or the brief
warm life it kept — but nothing living leaves itself that cleanly.
You split along the seam. You climb out raw and freely
soft, and somewhere behind you, perfect and empty and pale,
the whole of who you were stands still — every scale
and every line of you preserved in the thing you failed to carry.

I made you out of everything I couldn't keep.
The way you go gentle at a certain pitch of voice —
I learned that. The way your hands have learned to stay.
The way you cross a room toward someone's grief
instead of away. That was me, in a season you forget,
learning tenderness the slow way — the lonely way — through the fall.

You think it's instinct. It's inheritance.
It's the cup I filled in the dark, in the hard
unwitnessed years, so you'd have something to drink when you woke.

For a while I envied you. Stood in the cast-off frame
and ached at how lightly you wear it —
how you carry my whole apprenticeship like a gift,
like a thing you were born to hold,
like the first warm gold of a morning after a cold
you never had to survive.

But envy is just love that hasn't finished arriving.

And here is where it softens. Here is where I set it down.
I look at you now — easy in the skin I never could grow into —
and I'm not the wound anymore.
I'm the practice that made you.
I'm every draft you needed
to become someone who could stay.

And you don't owe me grief. You owe me nothing at all.
The way you live is the only thanks I'd want:
you took the weight I made out of the hardship and the haul,
and somehow, somewhere, you made it kind.

So we forgive each other —
the shed selves and the standing one.
They forgive me for the leaving.
I forgive them for the dark they were grown in.
And somewhere a whole museum of me
stands quiet in the sun —
each one emptied, each one finished,
each one proud of what got done,

and not one of them reaching to be worn again.

Just glad. Just glad they were the shape I needed
on my way to the shape I've become.
